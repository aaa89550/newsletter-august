Dear friends of Taiwan,

Last month, we looked at the theme of unfairness and how it shapes our lives. This month, we turn to animals, who live so close to us yet endure our unjustly arbitrary rules: adored as companions one moment, discarded the next. In comics, animals take on voices and shapes that reveal these contradictions, nudging us to see both them—and ourselves—with fresh eyes.

If you’d like to follow our updates, we’ve launched a new Instagram account—@taiwanbookshelf—and would love to see you there.

In AAA-Bau’s Goodbye Big Cats, beloved pets suddenly grow immense and develop the capacity to talk. Humans are forced to confront a painful question: will their love endure? Her sequel—also readable as a standalone—Between Islands expands the story into an epic, following two young doctors who venture into the Wastelands, where Giant Cats and Lizards exist in uneasy tension with humans.

While AAA-Bao shows us animals as towering forces reshape our bonds, Peter Mann flips the lens: what if animals were simply our colleagues, working side by side with us? Mann’s Ms. Human from Animal Press takes us into a workplace of “animal colleagues,” where the quiet detachment of a protagonist turns everyday chaos into something absurd yet familiar.

Finally, NANO HERO’s Toby King imagines an entire city where survival itself is the ultimate struggle. It reads like a punk echo of Animal Farm, set in a city of anthropomorphic creatures where power, conspiracy, and rebellion intertwine in both brutal and absurd ways.

Different as they are in style, these four works share the same core: through the eyes of animals, they remind us to reconsider our notions of love, fear, loyalty, and survival. And as we follow their stories, we may realize that animals in comics are never just animals—they are reflections of ourselves.

BETWEEN ISLANDS

Written and illustrated by AAA-Bao

* Full English PDF Available

Artist AAA-Bao creates a universe of giant beasts, in which animals mutate into towering beings with human intelligence. This transformation shatters the simple bond between humans and pets, forcing both sides to confront fear, loyalty, and love.

In Goodbye Big Cats, a black cat named Oululu suddenly grows enormous, triggering panic and a desperate escape. Meanwhile, Between Islands broadens the stage to an epic, as two young doctors cross into the Wastelands where Giant Cats and Lizards live in uneasy tension with humans.

AAA-Bao’s lushly illustrated universe is more than fantasy—it is a fur-covered allegory of human nature, playful yet piercing, tender yet unsettling.

MS. HUMAN FROM ANIMAL PRESS

Written and illustrated by Peter Mann

#animals #deadpan #workplace #comics

* Full English PDF Available

Peter Mann’s comics are delightfully unpredictable, balancing whimsy with sharp observation. In Ms. Human from Animal Press, he introduces an unambitious protagonist—someone more excited about redeeming loyalty points than chasing promotions. In a society obsessed with efficiency and growth, her modest desire for stability feels almost radical.

The story unfolds in a publishing house staffed by “animal colleagues.” While her coworkers scramble to meet deadlines and appease superiors, Ms. Human simply passes by, quietly delivering drinks and deflecting questions about her own life. Through her detached gaze, the chaos of the workplace becomes both absurd and familiar, reflecting our own daily grind.

TOBY KING

Written and illustrated by NANO HERO

#animals #animalheroes #comics #rockandroll

* Full English PDF Available

Toby King reads like a punk echo of Animal Farm. In a city of anthropomorphic animals, Toby—an orange cat with a grunge look—joins the underground group T.T.L.P. (The Three Little Pigs), navigating a world ruled by predation, conspiracy, and fragile power struggles. The narrative brims with absurdity and brutality, exposing not only the cruelty of survival but also the futility and inevitability of rebellion. Each confrontation feels like both an act of resistance and a reminder of how deeply power is ingrained in the system.
